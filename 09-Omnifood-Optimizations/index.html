Trigonometry is the branch of mathematics that explores the relationships between the angles and sides of triangles. It is essential for fields like engineering, physics, and astronomy, providing tools to solve real-world problems involving angles and distances.

How it works

Everything about trignometery in 3 simple steps

01

Definition and Basic Ratios:

Tigonometric ratios are relationships between the sides of a right-angled triangle. The primary ratios are sine (sin), cosine (cos), and tangent (tan). These ratios are defined as follows:
Sine: sin(θ) = Opposite side / Hypotenuse
Cosine: cos(θ) = Adjacent side / Hypotenuse
Tangent: tan(θ) = Opposite side / Adjacent side

02

How to Use Trigonometric Ratios:

To use trigonometric ratios, identify the angle of interest (θ) in a right-angled triangle. Depending on which sides you know (opposite, adjacent, or hypotenuse), you can use the appropriate ratio to find unknown sides or angles. For example, if you know the hypotenuse and the adjacent side, you can use the cosine ratio to find the angle.

03

Practical applications

Trigonometric ratios are used in various real-world applications such as navigation, architecture, and physics. For instance, they help in calculating heights of structures, distances in navigation, and analyzing wave patterns in physics. Understanding these ratios allows you to solve complex problems involving angles and distances efficiently.
